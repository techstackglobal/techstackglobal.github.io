The Rise of the "Wearable Ultrawide"

For years, AR glasses felt like expensive toys. You’d plug them in, the screen would drift, and the resolution felt like 2010. The XREAL 1S changes that. It's not trying to be a full VR headset like the Vision Pro; it wants to be your Ultrawide Monitor that fits in a sunglasses case.

Key Improvements in the 1S Model

The 1S revision introduces three critical features that make it a "winning product" in the 2026 market:

  • X1 Spatial Chip: Native 3DoF tracking. The screen stays locked in space even when you turn your head. No "floaty" UI.
  • Real-Time 3D: A dedicated hardware button toggles any 2D content (YouTube, Netflix) into an immersive 3D space.
  • 700 Nits Brightness: It's finally bright enough to use in a well-lit coffee shop without needing the blackout covers.

What We Loved

  • The X1 chip dramatically reduces motion sickness.
  • Crystal clear 120Hz refresh rate for gaming.
  • Lightweight (82g) for long work sessions.
  • Plug-and-play with almost any USB-C device.

The Trade-offs

  • Requires a wired connection (USB-C).
  • Can get warm near the temples after 2+ hours.
  • Audio is good, but Sony XM5s are still better.

Performance & Gaming

Pairing the XREAL 1S with a Steam Deck OLED is a revelation. Instead of squinting at a 7-inch screen, you're playing on a massive cinema display while lying on your couch. The input lag is virtually non-existent, making it perfect for fast-paced shooters or Elden Ring sessions.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does it work with iPhone 15/16/17?

Yes, any iPhone with a USB-C port works natively for screen mirroring. For full spatial apps, the XREAL Beam Pro is recommended.

Is it better than the Meta Quest 3?

For productivity and media consumption, yes. It's much lighter and clearer. For VR gaming (Beat Saber, etc.), the Quest is still superior.
